# Manufacturing

## Why this vertical fits
Manufacturing is one of the clearest fits for BlokSec because authentication often breaks exactly where the business cannot tolerate friction: the plant floor.

## Core pain
- Workers share terminals across shifts.
- Gloves, PPE, or clean-room controls make typing painful or impractical.
- One locked account can slow a line, delay a process, or trigger workarounds.
- IT and OT access patterns make credential accountability more important.

## Why passwords fail here
Passwords are slow, easy to share, hard to rotate at scale, and a bad fit for gloved or constantly moving workers. Phone-based MFA also fails when workers cannot use personal devices on the floor.

## Why BlokSec matters here
- fast worker switching on shared devices
- no dependency on employee smartphones
- stronger accountability than shared credentials
- better fit for regulated and audited environments
- easier replacement and re-provisioning than hardware-token-heavy models

## GTM angle
Lead with operational friction plus compliance:
- shared credentials create audit risk
- reset and lockout cycles waste production time
- frontline MFA cannot depend on phones
