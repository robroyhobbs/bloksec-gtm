# Care Settings

> **Audience:** Internal GTM use
> **Purpose:** Short GTM brief for care environments where PointClickCare pressure and shared-station workflows create a cleaner wedge

---

## Why this segment fits

Care settings are one of the clearest near-term wedges because four things collide:

- shared stations
- urgent workflows
- rotating shifts
- growing authentication pressure from platforms like PointClickCare

That matters because the default phone-centric MFA model fits poorly in exactly these environments.

---

## Core pain

- PointClickCare and similar platforms are increasing MFA urgency
- staff move quickly between shared stations
- personal-device MFA creates friction when workers do not reliably carry managed phones
- password sharing becomes the shortcut when speed matters
- lockouts and resets interrupt care
- many environments have lean IT and weak IAM maturity

---

## Why BlokSec matters here

- a better fit for shared-station workflows than generic phone-based MFA
- faster access on shared devices
- better accountability than shared credentials
- clearer audit trails
- less reset burden for lean IT teams

---

## GTM message

Lead with the workflow gap, not abstract cybersecurity language:

> PointClickCare and rising healthcare MFA pressure are forcing stronger authentication into environments where phone-based MFA does not fit. BlokSec gives care teams a badge-based path that works on shared stations, improves accountability, and reduces workflow friction.

Short version:

- shared stations
- no-phone workflows
- better accountability
- lighter deployment

---

## Best-fit environments

- assisted living
- skilled nursing
- memory care
- rehab
- smaller post-acute care environments

These are usually a better starting point than large hospital systems because the need is real but the deployment model is simpler.

---

## How to talk about it

Lead with workflow first:

- PointClickCare urgency is real, but its default model does not solve the frontline workflow cleanly
- fast secure access for staff on shared devices matters more than generic MFA language
- fewer workarounds during shift changes means better compliance and better care operations
- simpler re-issue and recovery matters in high-turnover environments

Then bring in compliance and auditability as supporting pressure.
