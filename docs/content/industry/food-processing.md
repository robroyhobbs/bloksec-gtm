# Food Processing

## Why this vertical fits
Food processing combines some of the hardest physical constraints for authentication: gloves, hygiene rules, controlled environments, and strict accountability.

## Core pain
- workers cannot casually remove gloves to type passwords
- hygiene rules create friction around shared touchpoints
- shifts are frequent and worker turnover can be high
- regulated operations need stronger traceability

## Why passwords fail here
Password-based access assumes workers can stop, type, recover, and repeat. In food environments, that assumption creates friction, delay, and unsafe workarounds.

## Why BlokSec matters here
- better fit for gloved work
- less friction at shared terminals
- stronger auditability than shared credentials
- easier credential replacement and re-issuance than hardware-heavy alternatives

## GTM angle
Lead with practical workflow fit:
- authentication that works in hygiene-constrained environments
- stronger accountability without slowing operations
- less reset burden for high-turnover crews

## Best supporting assets
- frontline worker research
- compliance context
- manufacturing and regulated-operations messaging
