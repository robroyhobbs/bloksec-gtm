# BlokSec Research Revisit — March 2026

## Executive summary
The core GTM thesis still works: BlokSec is strongest when positioned at the intersection of frontline workers, shared devices, compliance pressure, and password-reset pain. The best near-term buyers remain SMB and mid-market organizations with distributed workforces and limited IAM sophistication, reached through MSP and partner channels.

The market has also moved. BlokSec itself has progressed with `v4.0`, better admin tooling, improved reporting, clearer docs, and active publishing. At the same time, competitive pressure has increased, especially around frontline QR-based authentication inside the Microsoft ecosystem.

## What still holds
- The sharpest wedge is frontline and shared-device authentication.
- MSPs remain the best scaling channel for SMB and mid-market buyers.
- Compliance remains the strongest forcing function in retail, manufacturing, logistics, and regulated operations.
- Password resets, turnover, shift changes, and shared terminals are still the most practical door-openers.
- BlokBadge remains the clearest differentiator when the buyer cannot rely on employee smartphones.

## New learnings since the earlier research
- BlokSec now has a visible `v4.0` story with a rebuilt admin console, richer reporting, improved onboarding, and stronger documentation.
- The live site now includes product pages, case studies, blog activity, and release notes that did not exist in the older February research set.
- Microsoft Entra QR + PIN for frontline workflows is a more serious threat than the original materials reflected.
- Free-trial and partner-friction expectations are higher than before; trial, NFR, or guided proof options now matter more for GTM.
- Credibility now depends more on source quality than raw volume of statistics. Older weakly sourced numbers should be retired or reworked.

## Website audit highlights
### What is working
- The site now communicates a broader platform rather than a vague concept.
- `BlokKey`, `BlokBadge`, `case studies`, and `v4.0` create stronger product legitimacy.
- Release notes and docs add maturity and proof of product progress.

### What is weak or broken
- The homepage still speaks too broadly and does not lead with the strongest wedge.
- The ICP is not explicit enough for SMB and mid-market frontline-heavy buyers.
- `free-trial`, `demo`, `contact`, and `legal` appear to collapse into the same generic contact/newsletter experience.
- `BlokBadge` contains a copy bug (`With BlokKey`) that undermines trust.
- The case studies exist, but they do not yet create enough vertical-specific buyer proof.
- There is not enough direct language around password reset elimination, shared-workstation accountability, or instant re-provisioning after lost credentials.

## Best-fit target industries now
1. Manufacturing
2. Retail
3. Warehousing and logistics
4. Food processing
5. Care settings such as senior care, clinics, and distributed care environments

These all match the same pattern:
- shared or semi-shared devices
- shift changes and rapid worker switching
- high turnover or frequent onboarding
- PPE, hygiene, or workflow constraints that make traditional login painful
- compliance or audit pressure that makes shared credentials unacceptable

## Missing GTM assets
### Messaging and positioning
- A sharper frontline-first homepage narrative
- A clear response to Microsoft Entra QR + PIN
- A stronger narrative around lost-password elimination and instant re-provisioning
- A simpler explanation of where BlokSec complements, not replaces, existing identity stacks

### Industry assets
- Dedicated pages for manufacturing, retail, logistics, food processing, and care settings
- Industry-specific proof points and workflow examples
- Compliance pages mapped to actual buyer situations rather than generic regulation summaries

### Partner assets
- A single, aligned MSP program model
- Trial/NFR guidance
- Competitive battlecards
- Campaign-in-a-box materials for partners

### Proof and conversion
- Stronger case-study summaries with measurable before/after results
- Demo and trial paths that actually convert
- Clear outcomes around ticket reduction, login speed, compliance readiness, and worker onboarding

## Recommendation
Build the dedicated GTM repo around one public hub and one internal operating layer:
- public-facing GitHub Pages hub in `/docs`
- source context, research, and strategy in the same repo
- reusable partner, sales, and content assets linked from the hub

That gives BlokSec one place to update the story, expand industry messaging, and publish better proof without losing the underlying research base.
