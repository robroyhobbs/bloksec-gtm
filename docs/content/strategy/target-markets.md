# Target Markets: Manufacturing & Retail

## Why These Two First

1. **Compliance urgency** — PCI DSS 4.0 (retail) and FDA 21 CFR Part 11 (manufacturing) are active NOW
2. **Clear pain point** — Frontline workers in both verticals can't use phone-based MFA
3. **MSP alignment** — MSPs serving these verticals understand the problem immediately
4. **Healthcare deferred** — Difficult market entry, long sales cycles, will revisit later

## Manufacturing

### Market Size
- 12.8M workers in North America
- 40-60% annual turnover
- Average hourly wage: $22-28

### Pain Points
- Workers wear gloves — can't use touchscreens
- Clean room environments prohibit personal devices
- Shift changes require fast authentication (30 seconds, not 2 minutes)
- Shared terminals across the floor
- FDA requires unique electronic signatures for regulated processes

### Compliance Drivers
- **FDA 21 CFR Part 11:** Unique electronic signatures, 2-factor auth, audit trails
- **SOC 2:** Individual authentication, no shared accounts
- **Customer audit requirements:** Enterprise buyers auditing supplier security

### Buyer Profile
- **Title:** VP Operations, Plant Manager, IT Director, CISO
- **Trigger:** Failed audit, new customer requirement, breach at competitor
- **Budget:** Operational expense, often comes from IT or compliance budget
- **Decision process:** 2-4 months, needs IT + operations buy-in

### Acquisition Strategy
1. MSPs serving manufacturing SMBs (100-2,000 workers)
2. LinkedIn targeting: Manufacturing + compliance job titles
3. Content: FDA 21 CFR Part 11 compliance guide (lead magnet)
4. Trade associations: National Association of Manufacturers, SME
5. Breach newsjacking: When manufacturing companies get breached

## Retail

### Market Size
- 16M workers in North America
- 100-150% annual turnover (highest of any vertical)
- Average hourly wage: $15-19

### Pain Points
- Shared POS terminals — multiple workers per register
- Seasonal staffing surges (holiday, back-to-school)
- Workers don't have company-issued devices
- Onboarding speed is critical — can't spend 30 min on credential setup
- PCI DSS 4.0 now requires MFA for ALL cardholder data access

### Compliance Drivers
- **PCI DSS 4.0:** Universal MFA for all CDE access (active enforcement)
- **State privacy laws:** Increasing state-level requirements
- **Breach liability:** Payment card brands can fine merchants directly

### Buyer Profile
- **Title:** VP IT, Director of Loss Prevention, CISO, CIO
- **Trigger:** PCI audit finding, QSA recommendation, breach scare
- **Budget:** Security/compliance budget, sometimes loss prevention
- **Decision process:** 1-3 months, faster than manufacturing

### Acquisition Strategy
1. MSPs serving retail chains (5+ locations)
2. PCI QSA (Qualified Security Assessor) referral partnerships
3. Content: PCI DSS 4.0 MFA requirements guide (lead magnet)
4. Trade: National Retail Federation, Retail Industry Leaders Association
5. LinkedIn: Retail IT + compliance + loss prevention titles
6. Approach: "PCI DSS 4.0 is active — are your POS terminals compliant?"
