# Positioning & Messaging Framework

## Core Positioning Statement

BlokSec is the only passwordless authentication platform that covers your entire workforce — from the office to the factory floor — through a single platform that MSPs can deploy, manage, and profit from.

## Messaging by Audience

### For MSPs / Partners
> "BlokSec gives MSPs a white-label passwordless authentication platform that covers every worker — from office to frontline — so you can eliminate password reset tickets, add recurring revenue, and make your clients compliant."

### For IT Decision-Makers
> "BlokSec is the only passwordless platform that covers your entire workforce. BlokKey for office workers. BlokBadge for the floor. One platform, zero passwords, complete compliance."

### For Manufacturing Buyers
> "Your floor workers can't use phones. Traditional MFA doesn't work with gloves. BlokBadge gives every worker secure, auditable access with just a badge scan and PIN."

### For Retail Buyers
> "PCI DSS 4.0 requires MFA for every POS terminal. Your seasonal workers don't have company phones. BlokBadge gives them compliant authentication in 30 seconds — badge scan + PIN."

## Five Key Differentiators

1. **"Authentication for workers without phones"** — The one thing competitors can't do well
2. **"One platform, every worker"** — BlokKey (office) + BlokBadge (floor) = no gaps
3. **"No hardware to buy, no hardware to lose"** — QR badge is printed, not purchased
4. **"Immutable audit trail"** — Tamper-proof records, not just logs
5. **"Deploy in hours, not months"** — Standard protocols, no custom integration

## Brand Voice

**We sound like:** A trusted security advisor who respects your intelligence
**We don't sound like:** An aggressive vendor creating FUD (fear, uncertainty, doubt)

**Do:**
- Lead with the problem before the solution
- Use specific numbers and data points
- Acknowledge complexity honestly
- Show how we complement existing infrastructure

**Don't:**
- Use scare tactics or breach fear-mongering
- Claim to be "the only" solution (we work alongside Entra, Okta, etc.)
- Oversimplify security decisions
- Use jargon without explanation
