# Battlecard — Microsoft Entra QR + PIN

## Why this matters
This is the most important competitive response for BlokSec because it attacks the same frontline/shared-device workflow story.

## What Microsoft wins on
- existing Microsoft footprint
- bundled relationship with M365 and Entra buyers
- familiar admin surface for Microsoft-centric teams
- strong credibility for buyers already standardized on the Microsoft ecosystem

## Where BlokSec can still win
- mixed environments that are not purely Microsoft
- organizations needing a clearer cross-platform office + frontline story
- buyers who want BlokSec as a dedicated authentication layer rather than another Microsoft feature dependency
- teams that value a more explicit partner/MSP-led motion and differentiated frontline narrative

## How to position
Do not lead with `Microsoft cannot do this` unless the specific claim is proven.

Lead with:
- BlokSec works across mixed identity environments, not just one vendor ecosystem.
- BlokSec can be positioned as the simpler frontline-focused authentication layer for organizations with more than one stack reality.
- BlokSec can tell a stronger shared-device, worker-switching, and partner-delivered story than a bundled feature page.

## Questions to answer before hard external claims
- What frontline workflows does Entra now cover well enough to neutralize the old argument?
- Where does BlokSec still have practical workflow or deployment advantages?
- Which comparison points are truly product-level differences versus marketing-level differences?

## Sales talk track
`If you are all-in on Microsoft, Entra QR + PIN belongs in the conversation. But many SMB and mid-market teams are not living in a single-vendor world. BlokSec is strongest when you need a frontline-focused experience that fits mixed environments and still covers office workers too.`

## Proof still needed
- feature-by-feature comparison
- deployment friction comparison
- partner-management comparison
- real examples where BlokSec complements Entra rather than competes head-on
