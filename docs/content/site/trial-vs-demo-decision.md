# Trial vs Demo vs Guided Pilot

## Problem
The current `free-trial` path is ambiguous. For a security product with mixed workforce workflows, the site needs a clearer low-friction offer.

## Option 1: Demo-first
### Best when
- the sales motion is consultative
- the product still benefits from guided setup
- the team wants better qualification before investing time

### Pros
- easiest to support operationally
- strongest message control
- avoids putting a weak or half-ready trial on the site

### Cons
- higher friction for self-serve-minded buyers
- weaker signal for MSPs that expect hands-on evaluation access

## Option 2: Guided pilot
### Best when
- the product works best in a real customer workflow
- there is a clear success plan and evaluation path
- the team wants more seriousness than a generic free trial

### Pros
- stronger fit for security and frontline workflow evaluation
- easier to tie to buyer outcomes
- more realistic for mixed-office/frontline environments

### Cons
- more resource intensive than a simple demo
- may still feel heavy for smaller buyers

## Option 3: Limited free trial
### Best when
- onboarding is truly light
- the team can support self-serve evaluation
- the product experience is polished enough to stand alone

### Pros
- lowest friction
- useful for some partner and technical evaluations
- easier to convert curiosity into action

### Cons
- risky if the experience is not clean
- can create support load without producing qualified demand

## Option 4: Partner NFR plus customer demo
### Best when
- MSPs are the main scale channel
- the business wants to reduce friction for partners more than for every prospect

### Pros
- aligns with channel-first motion
- gives partners room to learn and sell
- more realistic than pretending the whole market should self-serve

### Cons
- does not solve direct-buyer self-serve expectations by itself

## Recommended near-term choice
Use a demo-first public motion with a guided-pilot path for qualified buyers and an NFR/evaluation path for MSPs.

That is the safest way to remove the current ambiguity without overpromising a self-serve free-trial experience the team may not actually want to support.
